Introduction

Welcome to the Argupedia user study homepage, thank you so much for taking the time to check out the applications and provide your critical thoughts and opinions! Use the navbars to navigate freely around the website.

The core purpose of the study is to collect your opinion via an anonymous survey on two totally different online debating applications - one the largest public repository of online debates called Debate.org and the other a conceptual prototype based on research within AI argumentation developed by myself at King's College London for my summer MSc Advanced Computer Science thesis. Ultimately the user study should be fun and interesting and you are successfully contributing towards critical research on the development of future web applications!

Please be sure to spend some time reading all below instructions and watch the respective YouTube videos before proceeding to testing and evaluating either debating platforms and filling out the surveys.

If you have any questions, thoughts or concerns at any point please do not hesitate to use the above link to send me an email.

Instructions

The user study is (hopefully) super simple and should only take 45 minutes to an hour at most. Read all the three sets of bullet-point instructions and follow them in order - ultimately a laptop or desktop computer is suitable to best experience both platforms, however if you choose to use a mobile or tablet, that is totally fine too!

Without further ado, turning to the bullet point instructions for Debate.org:

  • First, use the navbar link to navigate to the worlds largest online debate platform called Debate.org.
  • Spend some time on Debate.org familiarising yourself with the website and platform.
  • Secondly, try to spend some time interacting with Debate.org.
  • Interaction can mean reading some active debates, creating a Debate.org account and contributing to live debates, starting new debates, contributing to forums and polls.
  • Debate.org is open source and does not require an account - you can easily contribute anonymously.
  • If you are already an expereinced member of Debate.org that is totally fine and you can proceed to testing the Argupedia application.
  • Finally, after 20 to 45 minutes spent on Debate.org click on the Attrakdif survey link in the Navbar and navigate to filling out a survey on your experience and opinions of the Debate.org platform.

    • Instructions for the Attrakdif survey:

  • Use the navbar to reach the Attrakdif survey links.
  • Click on the correct link for either the Debate.org or the Argupedia multiple choice survey.
  • An Attrakdif survey should take roughly 3 to 5 minutes to complete.
  • The Attrakdif survey uses word pairs in which you rate the platform between two starkly contrasted words.
  • Do not ponder over the word pairs and make your assessment spontaneously - you may feel that some word pairs do not fit the product very well.
  • However, I would ask you to give an answer anyway. Remember at all times that there are no "right" or "wrong" answers - your personal opinion is what counts.

    • Lastly, instructions for the Argupedia platform:

  • Firstly, please watch the following YouTube video will hopefully make the intearction with the Argupedia platform totally clear.
  • Secondly, don't forget to use the navbar links to fill out the Attrakdif survey for Argupedia - it will be the exact same survey again!

    • Congratulations - you're all done give yourself a well-deserved pat on the back!

    About the study

    Research ethics and further information

    This study recieved ethical clearance from King's College London - it is classed as minimal ethical risk. My details can be used at any point if you have questions, complaints or withdrawal requests. Participants have the right to withdraw either during or after participation without having to provide a reaosn. All data collected from the surveys is deliberately totally anonymous and there is no way in which personal identifying details can be collected via the survey. By submitting a multiple choice Attrakdif survey you are providing informed consent that I may use your survey results as a part of my thesis - the survey results will be presented fully anonymously. The Attrakdif survey data results will be securely stored in line with the Data Protection Act (1998) and King's College London guidance.
