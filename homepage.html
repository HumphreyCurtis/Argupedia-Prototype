Introduction

Welcome to the Argupedia user study homepage, thank you so much for taking the time to check out the applications and provide your critical thoughts and opinions! Use the navbars to navigate freely around the website.

The core purpose of the study is to collect your opinion via an anonymous survey on two totally different online debating applications - one the largest public repository of online debates called Debate.org and the other a conceptual prototype based on research within AI argumentation developed by myself at Kings College London for my summer MSc Advanced Computer Science thesis. Ultimately the user study should be fun and interesting and you are successfully contributing towards critical research on the development of future web applications!

Please be sure to spend some time reading all below instructions and watch the respective YouTube videos before proceeding to testing and evaluating either debating platforms and filling out the surveys.

If you have any questions, thoughts or concerns at any point please do not hesitate to use the above link to send me an email.

Instructions

The user study is (hopefully) super simple and should only take an hour at most. Read all the three sets of bullet-point instructions and follow them in order - ultimately a laptop or desktop computer is suitable to best experience both platforms, however if you choose to use a mobile or tablet, that is totally fine too!

Without further ado, turning to the bullet point instructions for Debate.org:

  • First, use the navbar link to navigate to the worlds largest online debate platform called Debate.org.
  • Spend some time on Debate.org familiarising yourself with the website and platform.
  • Secondly, try to spend some time interacting with Debate.org.
  • Interaction can mean reading some active debates, creating a Debate.org account and contributing to live debates, starting new debates, contributing to forums and polls.
  • Debate.org is open source and does not require an account - you can easily contribute anonymously.
  • If you are already an expereinced member of Debate.org that is totally fine and you can proceed to testing the Argupedia application.
  • Thirdly and lastly, after 20 to 45 minutes spent on Debate.org click on the Attrakdif survey link in the Navbar and navigate to filling out a survey on your experience and opinions of the Debate.org platform.

    The vaccination chloropleth maps display divided geographical areas or regions are coloured, shaded or patterned in relation to the variable selected by the user.

    Each vaccination chloropleth comes with 4 optional metrics choosable via the dropdown (all mathematic calculations are rounded to 3 decimal places):

  • Total vaccinations - Total number of vaccinations used by each nation for their population.
  • People vaccinated - Total number of people to recieve at least one vaccination within each nation.
  • Total vaccinated proportionally - To gain a proportional metric of each nation's vaccination rollout with: (Total vaccinated in current nation / Total vaccinated worldwide) x 100
  • People vaccinated proportionally - To gain a proportional metric of each nation's vaccination rollout with: (People vaccinated in current nation / People vaccinated worldwide) x 100
